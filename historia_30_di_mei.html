Workers' dissatisfaction grew as wages stagnated and working conditions deteriorated. The labor unions, which represented the working class, increasingly called for better wages and conditions, but their pleas were largely ignored by both the government and employers.

On May 30th, 1969, the discontent reached a boiling point. Workers from the oil refinery, joined by other dissatisfied citizens, marched through the streets of Willemstad. The initially peaceful protest quickly turned violent as clashes broke out between the protesters and the police.

The aftermath of 30 di Mei was profound. The events led to significant political changes, including the resignation of the government. The uprising also brought attention to the dire conditions faced by the working class and led to improvements in labor laws and wages.
